How religious lobbying can influence the law you have to obey

Religious organisations spent tens of millions of euros in the last decade to shape European Union policy and law making, according to an analysis of datasets and reports by the European Commission and Parliament.

Since the creation of the EU, these institutions lobbied by investing money and time on meeting with members of parliament (+campaigns) to advice them on topics that are relevant to update or change the law.

In the last three years, thousands of religious and non religious bodies spent 2 billion euros on lobbying in the the European Union, according to the EU Transparency Register.

It is legal for Churches and their affiliated organizations to spend money on lobbying and they are taking this opportunity to address a wide variety of issues and to lobby against legal matter practices like abortion and same-sex marriage.

The main sucessful religious campaigns

The professionalization of religious lobbying coincides with their participation in new debates as the climate change or immigration. But Christian lobbyists’ main fight is and will likely remain what we call the Sexual and Reproductive Health and Right (SRHR). This includes abortion, families right, same-sex marriage… When it comes to these topics, they often unify to try to influence the EU law. Indeed, since 2010, they had some significant victories.

A, B, C V IRELAND

Goal: Be allowed to travel to the UK to be aborted because abortions are unlawful in Ireland

Who was involved: three women against the Irish law

methods used: Go in front of European Court of Human Right

ONE OF US CAMPAIGN

Goal: stop EU funding for any activities involving the destruction of the human embryo 

Who was involved: One of Us Federation - COMECE (Conferences of the European Community) - ECPM (European Christian Political Movement)

methods used: Inform and mobilize the public opinion thanks to active social media campaigns

EDITE ESTRELA REPORT

Goal: Block a pro-choice report promoting safer abortion and xomen's rights

Who was involved: European Dignity Watch - FAFCE

methods used: mass emailing, battle of informatio, social media campaigns

#DEFUNDIPPF

Goal: Defund IPPF organisation

Who was involved: One of Us - European Diginty Watch - ADF International

methods used: Social media campaigns, battle of information

Most of these campaigns were successful for the religious campaigners either by their importance or their result.

The Edite Estrela report aimed at passing a law to facilitate abortion but the EP finally decided that these decisions should be made by the member states at a national level.

The ‘One of Us’ campaigns started as a European citizens initiative then backed by churches. Their petition to stop EU funding of activities destructing human embryo gathered 1.5 million signatures online. Although their request has been turned down by the European Commission, they are bringing this decision to the European Court of Justice.

The A, B, C v Ireland was a victory for Ireland. The European Court of Justice considered that Ireland constitution doesn’t confer a right to abortion except when it could be life-threatening for the future mother.

#DefundIPPF didn’t lead to the expected result of defunding this organisation, nonetheless, it gave the opportunity to religious lobbyists, affiliated organisations and MEPs to unify and campaign together to try to interfere.

These campaigns, successful or not are strong example of the power of lobbyists when they unify.
